# Vision

**Wes is a living coach for the highest-leverage skill you have — communication — that gets better at coaching *you* the more of your real communication it sees, and can prove whether you're actually improving.**

Everything in this repo is in service of that sentence. When a decision is unclear, this is the tiebreaker.

## The load-bearing ideas

1. **A coach, not a tool.** Grammarly fixes *this sentence*. Wes is trying to change *you*. The unit of value is behavior change over time — like a tennis coach or a trainer, not a one-off edit. The product is the loop: **diagnose → drill → track progress.**

2. **The asset is your blind spots, revealed at scale.** A coach doesn't learn your voice to mimic it — that would entrench the exact habits it should fix. It learns *what you keep getting wrong*, and where. You can't see that in one message; a hundred of your real messages expose the pattern. This is why Wes has to **watch**, not just be pasted into. (See [`docs/capture-channels.md`](docs/capture-channels.md) for the LEARN-vs-COACH split this implies.)

3. **It lives everywhere you communicate — or it can't see the pattern.** Slack, email, calls. Not for surface area's sake: the diagnosis is only as honest as the sample. That forces the hard constraint — ambient but **local-first, opt-in, redacted**. Trust is the price of ubiquity. (See [`docs/all-living-coach.md`](docs/all-living-coach.md) § Privacy.)

4. **Embodied in a master with a method, not generic AI.** Wes's frameworks (sales-then-logistics, MOO, accurate confidence…) give it a point of view and a vocabulary. It's "what would Wes say," and it names your miss in her terms — which is what makes the lesson stick and transfer.

5. **Success is you needing it less.** Wes's own principle. The best coach works itself out of a job, and the proof is in *your* data trending up — not in engagement.

## What this is not

- **Not a ghostwriter / voice clone.** It coaches you off your habits, it doesn't reproduce them. Voice-preservation is a small guardrail on rewrites (so you'll actually send them), never the goal.
- **Not spellcheck.** The value is longitudinal and diagnostic, not per-sentence correctness.
- **Not a paste-in chatbot.** That's surface #1, not the product. The product watches over time.

## Where it leads

The shape is a template: **capture real behavior → diagnose it against a master's frameworks → coach in-context → track improvement.** Communication is the wedge — universal, and the highest-leverage skill a founder has. The same architecture (markdown character + channel feeders + diagnosis + gateways) is how you'd build an embodied coach for any skill that shows up in your daily work.

## The bet — and the risk

**The bet:** longitudinal, diagnostic, in-your-workflow coaching is a different *category* from both spellcheck and a paste-in chatbot. Being watched over time and told your patterns is what actually moves behavior.

**The risk is entirely trust and willingness:** people have to let it watch, and be willing to look at what it finds. So the build order is trust-first — useful while opt-in and triggered, earning the right to go ambient.
